## What I Built With AI: A Personal Career Companion on My Own Website

I recently added a custom AI chat to my website — not because I wanted to chase a trend, but because I wanted to solve a real problem: how do I give someone a window into my background, ideas, and approach without writing a wall of text?

The answer was an AI career companion that lives right on jpartney.space. It connects to a Hugging Face Space hosted on Hugging Face, pulls context from my Idea Garden posts, and answers questions about who I am, what I think about, and where I'm headed. I have to give credit to Ed Donner for the inspiration. The project I built evolved from one that he created in one of his AI courses on udemy. I built it out further and adapted it to my needs.

### Why this matters

Most people talk about AI in the extremes of it being a threat to humanity or the greatest thing on earth or it's just going to take everyone's jobs. The reality I've discovered is more practical: AI is a completely new kind of tool that lets one person build things that previously required a team. I didn't need to hire a developer to put an intelligent chat experience on my site. I needed a clear idea, the right tools, and the willingness to iterate and learn...

### The human-centered angle

This solution isn't here to replace conversation — it's here to start one. It gives visitors a low-friction way to understand my perspective before reaching out. For me, it's a filter that helps the right people find me faster. That's the kind of leverage AI enables when you point it at your own specific problem.

### Where this could go

The same pattern applies to any business or personal project. You know your strengths, your gaps, and your goals better than any off-the-shelf tool designer ever could. The question isn't whether you're a developer — it's whether you're willing to learn how to build something that only you can build.
