## Personal AI representatives need trust before scale

A personal AI representative is only useful if people understand what it is — and what it is not.

It should not pretend to be the person. It should disclose that it is AI, stay within approved context, and invite better questions rather than trying to close every loop by itself.

For me, the interesting part is not just the chatbot. It is the system around it: the ideas it can reference, the workflows it can trigger, and the way it helps someone engage with a person’s work more deeply before a real conversation happens.

That is why this page is intentionally experimental. It is a small public place to test how AI can become more personal, more useful, and still remain honest about its limits.
