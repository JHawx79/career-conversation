## What Problem Are You Trying to Solve with AI?

I watched this video today by Dan Martell and it really hit home.

He talks about something I've been thinking a lot about lately: AI is a tool. If you think it's just going to automate your business and do everything for you, you're mistaken. It only works when you use it to accelerate something that already has solid fundamentals.

Dan shares a framework called the GAIN Matrix that I found really useful for thinking about where AI actually fits in your business:

**Give** — Easy for humans, easy for computers. Data entry, simple calculations. Give these to AI. Nobody should be doing them manually anymore.

**Accelerate** — Hard for humans, easy for computers. Deep research, analysis, pattern finding. This is where the real leverage is. AI does in minutes what used to take hours.

**Integrate** — Hard for both. Creative strategy, innovation. This is where you and AI collaborate to co-create things neither could do alone.

**No AI** — Hard for computers, easy for humans. EQ, leadership, showing up for people. This is where humanity shines and AI has no place.

That framework lines up with how I want to approach this.

My role in this space isn't about building tools or selling products. It's about solving problems. And when I talk to someone looking to use AI, I want to start with the right questions:

What problem are you trying to solve? What process is creating friction? How can you become more efficient given what you already know about your business and your workflows?

Just like a skilled banker asks the right questions before recommending a solution, I'm doing the same now — understanding your pain points, then finding the first steps to simplify a process, improve a workflow, or save time by automating something repetitive.

All areas that weren't really explored before, and now need to be with AI.

Big thanks to Dan Martell for the inspiration on this one. Solid, no-nonsense advice.
