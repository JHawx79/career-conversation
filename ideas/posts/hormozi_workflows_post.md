# Draft: Think in Workflows, Not Hierarchies

Today, most business decisions are made the way the Romans ran legions.

Pyramid structures. Top-down reporting layers of managers between the person(s) with the original ideas and directives and the people who make it slowly a reality the further down the chain of command. That model hasn't changed.

Alex Hormozi, one of my favorite content creators right now, describes how all businesses fundamentally work by contrasting this archaic structure system with the process of getting customers (first step) to eventually buy what you sell (final step) and everything in between as a workflow: successful business leaders today *"...think in workflows. Less headcount. More output. Better results."*

Let's compare:

**Old model — Traditional Hierarchy**
Ideas flow up. Decisions flow down. Every decision passes through multiple layers before reaching the people who do the actual work. The org chart is the map.

**New model — Workflow Oriented**
Work flows horizontally. A process starts at point A, moves through defined stages, and delivers a result at point B. The workflow is the map.

What makes this effective?

> What do you sell, who do you sell to, and what workflows bridge the two?

When you answer that honestly, your entire business structure is exposed as a process. You stop hiring for titles and start building for outcomes. You stop adding layers and start removing friction. You define constraints, leverage, best practices, and solutions become more apparent.

Here's a simple reframe - Build your business like an aqueduct, not a pyramid.
