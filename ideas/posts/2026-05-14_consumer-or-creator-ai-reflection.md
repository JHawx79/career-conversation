## Consumer or Creator? Where I Stand on AI Right Now

A couple of years ago, I was pretty skeptical about AI.

For me, the turning point wasn't some abstract fear — it was Microsoft Recall. When Windows 11 started quietly screenshotting everything I did on my screen to feed into an AI memory system, something clicked. I didn't like the idea of a major company using my private data without me meaning to hand it over. It felt like an invasion.

But here's the ironic thing: that discomfort triggered a chain of events that led me straight into discovering local, private AI.

I started digging. I taught myself how AI actually works under the hood. I learned about running models locally, about privacy-preserving setups, about the whole ecosystem that exists outside the big tech walled gardens. And along the way, something shifted inside me.

I stopped being just a consumer of AI and started becoming a creator *with* AI.

And that shift matters. A lot.

When you're only consuming what AI produces, it's easy to feel passive — like something is being done *to* you. But when you start creating alongside AI, the whole experience flips. There's awe, curiosity, creativity. It's freeing. One path leaves you feeling like a spectator. The other puts you in the driver's seat.

So here's my question to you — and to my future self: **what are you actually doing with AI?**

Are you creating with it? Or just consuming what it's made for you?

I'm writing this now as a mile marker. A barometer, if you will. Because the sentiment around AI has shifted dramatically in just the last year. We went from people not even knowing what AI is to the hype being everywhere — and honestly, a little exhausting.

I don't know where things will be in three months. But I do know I want to stay intentional about how AI shapes my life. That's partly why I started this little project — a space to document my thoughts, grow my communication skills, and connect with people who share my values around privacy, local AI, and practical implementation.

I'm not expecting anything huge to happen right away. But I'm grateful for the chance to learn, grow, and figure out what it looks like to be someone who creates *with* AI instead of just consuming what it serves up.

I'd love to hear where you land on this. Are you creating, consuming, or somewhere in between?
