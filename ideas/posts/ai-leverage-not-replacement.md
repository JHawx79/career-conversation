## The Value of AI Is Leverage, Not Replacement

_Date: 2026-05-12_

Recent projects have reminded me of something important: the real value of AI is not simply automation. It is leverage.

When I try to make AI systems fully autonomous, especially with local models, I still see places where agent loops stumble over themselves. They can get repetitive, miss context, or overcomplicate a task that still needs human judgment. API-based models are getting very powerful, and local models are improving quickly, but the gap is still noticeable.

That has been frustrating at times. But it has also clarified the lesson.

### Automation is not the whole point

It is easy to talk about AI as if the goal is to remove the person from the process. In some narrow workflows, that may be useful. But in the work I care most about — business, finance, strategy, communication, and problem solving — the human element is not a bug. It is the point.

The better question is not:

> “How do I get AI to do everything for me?”

The better question is:

> “How do I use AI to create more leverage for the parts of the work that still require intent, taste, judgment, and trust?”

### Leverage still needs direction

This page is a small example of that.

I had an idea for a more personal and interactive career page. AI helped move quickly: shaping the layout, refining language, organizing sections, and turning a rough vision into something tangible. But the outcome still required human direction.

It needed my preferences.  
It needed my edits.  
It needed my sense of what felt too generic, too polished, too cluttered, or too impersonal.  
It needed me to describe the outcome with intent.

That is where the leverage showed up.

AI did not replace the creative or strategic process. It accelerated it.

### The human-centered lesson

The more I work with AI, the more convinced I become that the best use cases are not the ones that erase people from the loop. They are the ones that help people think better, communicate clearer, move faster, and build things they might not have had the time or confidence to build otherwise.

That is the version of AI I am most interested in: not just automation, but amplification.

Not replacing judgment, but extending it.  
Not removing personality, but helping express it more clearly.
