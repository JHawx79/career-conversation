## LTV to CAC: The One Ratio That Compresses Your Entire Business

Every business does a lot of things. Marketing, sales, product development, customer service, operations, hiring, infrastructure. It's easy to get lost in the complexity and lose sight of what actually matters.

There's one number that ties it all together: your Lifetime Value to Customer Acquisition Cost ratio.

### What it is

LTV is how much profit a customer generates over their entire relationship with you. CAC is how much you spend to bring that customer in the door. Divide one by the other, and you get a ratio that tells the whole story of your business in a single line.

A ratio above 1 means you're creating value. Below 1 means you're burning it. The higher it is, the more efficiently your business converts effort into profit.

### Why it works

This isn't a novel concept. It's not a framework invented by consultants. It's accounting — the kind that's been true for as long as businesses have existed. You spend money to attract customers, those customers pay you over time, and the gap between those two numbers is what keeps the lights on.

What makes LTV:CAC powerful is how much it compresses. It doesn't just measure marketing effectiveness. It measures:

- **Product quality** — does your offering retain customers?
- **Pricing strategy** — are you capturing enough value?
- **Customer experience** — do people stay, or do they leave?
- **Operational efficiency** — can you serve customers profitably?
- **Market fit** — are you solving a problem people will pay for?

Every department in your organization ultimately pushes or pulls on this ratio. That's why it's the fundamental unit of business profit.

### The fundamentals still win

I've found that focusing on fundamentals produces better results than chasing trends. LTV:CAC isn't flashy. It won't get you applause at a conference. But it works because it's rooted in how value actually flows through a business.

When you understand your ratio, you understand your business. When you improve it, you improve everything.
